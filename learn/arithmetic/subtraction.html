Subtraction

Simple (Grades 1–5) | Higher-Level (Grades 6+)

Concept

Subtraction is the process of taking one number away from another to find the difference. It helps you determine how much is left or how far apart numbers are.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Identify the number you are starting with (the minuend).
  2. Identify the number you are subtracting (the subtrahend).
  3. Subtract the subtrahend from the minuend.
  4. For multi-digit numbers, subtract column by column from right to left (ones, tens, hundreds), borrowing if needed.

Hands-On Techniques

  • Use fingers to count backwards for small numbers.
  • Draw tally marks or dots and cross out the amount being subtracted.
  • Use paper columns for multi-digit subtraction and borrow when necessary.
  • Break numbers apart for mental subtraction. Example: 53 − 27 = (50 − 20) + (3 − 7) = 30 − 4 = 26.

Worked Examples

Example 1 (Simple): 9 − 4 = 5
Count backwards: Start at 9, count down 4 steps → Total left = 5

Example 2 (Using Fingers): 7 − 3
Hold up 7 fingers, fold down 3 → Remaining fingers = 4

Example 3 (Column Subtraction): 84 − 37

          8 4
        - 3 7
        ----
          4 7
Subtract ones: 4 − 7 → borrow 1 from tens → 14 − 7 = 7
Subtract tens: 7 − 3 = 4 → Total = 47

Example 4 (Breaking Apart Numbers): 62 − 29
Break into tens and ones: (60 − 20) + (2 − 9) = 40 − 7 = 33

Practice

Try solving these problems using the methods above: 12 − 7, 34 − 18, 56 − 29, 89 − 47.
